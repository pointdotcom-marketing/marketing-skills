# Legal And Disclosure Planning

Use this as production planning guidance, not legal advice or a substitute for Point Legal approval.

## Observed baseline

A recent Point paid-social end frame reviewed in July 2026 used this on-asset footer:

- `Visit point.com for more information`
- Equal Housing Opportunity icon and text
- `Terms and conditions apply.`
- `NMLS #1610752`

Point's current licenses page also identifies NMLS #1610752. Treat this as an observed baseline to propose in briefs, not as immutable boilerplate. Confirm the exact copy, marks, and applicability with current approved assets or Legal before production.

Use this draft placeholder when current copy is unavailable:

```text
[Legal-approved footer: point.com information line + Equal Housing Opportunity + terms qualification + current NMLS identifier]
```

Do not silently recreate the Equal Housing Opportunity mark or any third-party badge. Request an approved asset.

## Separate three disclosure layers

### Baseline company and licensing footer

Plan for the company or destination reference, current NMLS identifier, Equal Housing Opportunity treatment when applicable, and general terms qualification. Legal decides which elements are required for the product, claim, placement, geography, and campaign.

### Claim-specific qualification

Flag claims that may need nearby qualification or a numbered footnote, including:

- amounts, ranges, rates, fees, or costs
- timing or speed
- credit-score or credit-impact language
- monthly-payment language
- term length
- income, equity, property, state, or underwriting requirements
- repayment, lien, appreciation, draw-period, or product-mechanics statements
- comparisons, savings, performance, or customer-outcome claims

Do not solve a disclosure problem by shrinking the copy until it is technically present but unreadable. Simplify the claim, allocate a dedicated legal area or end card, or route to a Legal-approved destination and qualification pattern.

### Optional trust proof

BBB ratings, Trustpilot scores, review counts, awards, and similar marks are proof elements, not baseline legal copy. A reviewed 2026 frame showed BBB `A+` and Trustpilot `Excellent 4.7`, but these values can change.

Before using them, verify the current value, approved badge artwork, trademark or platform requirements, date or review-count qualifiers, and Legal approval. Use `[current approved rating]` until confirmed.

## Testimonials and UGC

Confirm the speaker's identity, release, approved quote, editing permissions, attribution, state or location treatment, and whether the experience is typical or needs qualification. Do not invent, composite, paraphrase, or materially clean up a customer statement without approval.

For creator or actor UGC, disclose sponsorship or material connection when required by the platform, agreement, or Legal. Do not present scripted talent as an actual Point customer.

## Placement rules

- Reserve a legal-safe area during concepting, not after design is complete.
- Keep required copy inside every platform safe zone and away from controls or cropping.
- Use sufficient contrast, size, spacing, and display time for normal viewing on the intended device.
- Keep legal text static when the main motion would reduce readability.
- For video, specify whether the footer persists or appears on a dedicated end card.
- Rework every cutdown and ratio; do not assume the master disclosure survives trimming or resizing.
- Preserve approved copy exactly. Do not abbreviate or rewrite it to make the layout fit.
- Include required alt text, captions, and accessibility checks where the placement supports them.

## Brief checklist

Record:

- product and geography
- placement and aspect ratio
- baseline footer source and approval date
- each claim and its source
- claim-specific disclosure or footnote
- testimonial, rating, badge, and asset permissions
- legal-safe area and display duration
- owner for Legal review
- status: draft, submitted, approved, or rejected

Never mark a concept production-ready while required legal copy, permissions, or current-claim sources remain unresolved. Label it `creative-ready, pending Legal` when the concept is otherwise complete.
