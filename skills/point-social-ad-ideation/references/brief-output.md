# Social Ad Ideation And Brief Output

Use the smallest output that satisfies the request. Keep early ideation compact; expand only selected concepts.

## Concept territory card

- Name
- Homeowner tension
- Insight
- Premise
- First-frame hook
- Product truth
- Format and visual device
- Emotional payoff
- Proof or approved-proof placeholder
- CTA
- Primary test variable
- Claim and disclosure dependencies
- Production dependencies

## Production-ready brief

### Assignment

- Objective
- Audience
- Funnel stage
- Product
- Channel and placement
- Priority and timing
- Success signal

### Concept

Explain the homeowner situation, creative device, product truth, intended takeaway, and tone. Make the concept understandable without a mood board.

### Hook and message

- First-frame or first-three-seconds hook
- Primary message
- Supporting proof
- CTA
- Proposed headline, on-screen text, or VO roles

Label proposed copy as draft. Use `[approved claim]`, `[approved testimonial]`, or `[Legal-approved copy]` when the source is unconfirmed.

### Format and deliverables

Specify the format, aspect ratios, placements, master length, independent cutdowns, silent-first and caption requirements, source or clean versions, and optimization needs.

Treat 9:16 and 1:1 as common considerations, not automatic requirements. Match the actual media plan.

### Visual and production direction

Describe the scene, talent, interface, or object; what moves and remains fixed; pacing; mobile hierarchy; legal-safe area; required assets; and likely dependencies.

For animation, state how the loop begins and ends. For UGC, state who owns casting, filming, edit, graphics, captions, and implementation.

### Draft creative asset and design direction

Turn the concept into a concrete first-pass visual direction. Include:

- a rough composition or frame-by-frame layout
- headline, supporting copy, proof, CTA, logo, and legal hierarchy
- imagery, illustration, UI, talent, props, or footage direction
- typography, color, spacing, and graphic-device roles grounded in current Point brand guidance
- motion, transition, or editing behavior when relevant
- adaptation notes for each requested ratio, placement, and cutdown
- approved source assets needed, plus any permissions or validation still pending

When visual-generation or layout tools are available and the user wants a visual, create a rough key frame, storyboard, or low-fidelity mockup. Otherwise provide an unambiguous text wireframe or shot list that a designer can execute. Keep the draft legible at mobile size and reserve real space for the disclosure plan.

Call the output `draft creative direction`, `rough mockup`, or `representative key frame`. Do not present generated imagery, placeholder UI, draft copy, unverified badges, or reconstructed legal marks as final approved assets.

### Sequence

For each beat, specify timing or order, visual action, dialogue or VO, on-screen text, legal content, and purpose. Build each cutdown as a complete miniature story.

### References and source assets

List what to borrow from each reference: pacing, composition, transition, motion restraint, creator tone, or another specific quality.

Inventory approved logos, product UI, illustrations, testimonials, footage, legal copy, badges, and type treatments. Do not assume a flattened source can be edited element by element.

### Legal and disclosure plan

Follow [legal-disclosure-planning.md](legal-disclosure-planning.md). Include:

- observed or Legal-approved baseline footer
- claim-specific disclosure needs
- rating, testimonial, or badge validation
- placement, contrast, safe area, and screen time
- legal owner and approval status

### Guardrails

Include concise `Do` and `Do not` lists for product accuracy, homeowner tone, comparison fairness, motion, visual brand, prohibited implications, permissions, and legal review.

### Test plan

Define the hypothesis, primary variable, control, versions, intended learning, and metric supplied by the media or growth team. Change one primary strategic variable per test when possible.

## Ranking rubric

Use a 1–5 scale when ranking is helpful:

| Criterion | Question |
| --- | --- |
| Clarity | Can a homeowner understand the idea quickly without sound? |
| Relevance | Does it start from a recognizable need? |
| Distinctiveness | Is it strategically different from the other concepts? |
| Brand fit | Does it feel calm, clear, practical, and empowering? |
| Safety | Can the message be supported without overpromising? |
| Legal fit | Can required disclosures remain legible in every placement and cutdown? |
| Feasibility | Can the maker execute it with available assets, time, and budget? |
| Adaptability | Can it become intentional cutdowns and placements? |
| Learning value | Does the test answer a useful question? |

Do not let a total score hide a fatal issue. Repair unsupported claims, missing permissions, unreadable legal copy, or infeasible dependencies before advancing.
