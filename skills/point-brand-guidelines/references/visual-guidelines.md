# Point Visual Guidelines Reference

Source: [Point Brand Guide](https://files.point.com/Point-brand-guidelines.pdf), reviewed July 17, 2026.

Use the explicit rules below as requirements. Treat the artwork, photography, and design-pattern notes as visual direction inferred from examples because those pages contain examples without written rules.

## Logo

### Explicit requirements

- Do not change, alter, or adjust the original colors of the logo or other branded elements.
- Avoid backgrounds that clash with the logo or reduce its visibility.
- Use a minimum logo height of 20 px on digital platforms and screens.
- Use a minimum logo height of 0.6 in in print.
- Maintain clear space on all sides equal to at least 50% of the logo's rendered height.

Examples:

- A 40 px-high logo requires at least 20 px of clear space on every side.
- A 1.2 in-high logo requires at least 0.6 in of clear space on every side.

### Application guidance

- Select an approved full-color or monochrome logo asset that maintains contrast with the background.
- Preserve the supplied aspect ratio and artwork exactly.
- Do not redraw the wordmark, roof mark, or letter spacing.
- When an approved asset is unavailable, request it from Point's media kit or the brand team at `media@point.com`; do not approximate it.

## Typography

### Explicit requirements

- Set primary headers in ITC Century Book.
- Permit Circular Bold for smaller headers.
- Use normal weight for headers except headers set in Circular.
- Set all header line height to 150% (1.5em).
- Align headers left or center.
- Keep headers short and to the point.
- Set body copy in Circular Std Book.
- Use Circular Std Medium, Bold, and Black as available weights for hierarchy and emphasis.
- Use Lora Italic when an italic serif treatment is needed.

### Application guidance

- Use licensed font files provided by Point.
- Do not silently replace ITC Century Book, Circular Std, or Lora Italic with lookalikes. If the fonts are unavailable, identify the missing dependency and mark any fallback as temporary.
- Preserve readable sizing and spacing even when the output format imposes constraints.

## Artwork Direction

The guide provides examples rather than explicit written rules. The examples consistently suggest:

- Use muted blues, blue-grays, and charcoal as the base palette.
- Add yellow or orange as a restrained focal accent.
- Favor editorial vector illustration with simple geometry, soft glow, gradients, and tactile grain or texture.
- Connect imagery to home, money, security, paperwork, growth, and everyday milestones.
- Balance warmth and optimism with a calm, trustworthy financial tone.
- Keep compositions uncluttered with generous negative space.

Use this direction to select or commission approved artwork. Do not extract the examples from the PDF and treat them as licensed production assets.

## Photography Direction

The guide provides examples rather than explicit written rules. The examples consistently suggest:

- Show real people in recognizable home and financial-life settings.
- Represent a broad range of ages, races, households, and life stages.
- Favor candid, human moments such as cooking, researching, reviewing bills, speaking with an advisor, moving, or improving a home.
- Use natural or warm light and lived-in environments instead of glossy luxury staging.
- Let subjects retain agency; avoid imagery that shames or sensationalizes financial stress.
- Use occasional home-related still life when people are unnecessary.

Use approved or properly licensed photography. Treat these observations as art direction, not permission to reuse the PDF's sample images.

## Design Patterns

The guide's comparison-table examples suggest:

- Use a very light gray field with thin separators and generous white space.
- Highlight Point's column as a white, slightly elevated card.
- Center the Point logo or product name at the top of the highlighted column.
- Use Point Blue for Point checks or selected values and neutral gray for other entries.
- Keep row labels left aligned and values easy to scan across columns.
- Preserve clarity when adapting the table to mobile; do not rely on color alone to communicate a check, selection, or advantage.

The facts shown in these examples are historical mockup content. Do not copy amounts, term lengths, loan-to-value values, state counts, or competitor comparisons into production work without current approved sources.
