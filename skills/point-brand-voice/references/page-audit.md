# Point Main Site Voice Audit

Source pages audited:

- `https://point.com/`
- `https://point.com/hei`
- `https://point.com/heloc`

## Overall Pattern

Point's public pages position the company as a trusted home wealth partner for homeowners who want more financial flexibility. The copy balances aspiration with explanation: it talks about breathing easier, building a future, and pursuing goals, then grounds those ideas in concrete product mechanics.

The best recurring voice traits are:

- Clear: product mechanics are usually explained in direct, plain language.
- Empowering: homeowners are told they have options and control.
- Transparent: pages compare products, requirements, payments, terms, and repayment structure.
- Reassuring: customer proof and process descriptions reduce anxiety.
- Practical: benefits are tied to debt payoff, home repairs, medical bills, business, education, and retirement.

## Homepage Observations

Representative language:

- "Unlock your home equity. Build your future."
- "Get up to $600k of your home equity, your way."
- "Choose maximum flexibility and no monthly payments with a Home Equity Investment (HEI), or faster funding and a low rate with a HELOC."
- "Point makes home wealth more valuable for homeowners."
- "You built your home equity with every mortgage payment and renovation."
- "Point is your partner in home wealth. When you do well, we do well."

Voice takeaways:

- Leads with a broad homeowner aspiration, then immediately narrows to product choice.
- Uses contrast to help the homeowner understand fit: HEI for flexibility and no monthly payments, HELOC for speed and low rate.
- Positions Point as a partner, not just a lender.
- Uses "your way", "you decide", and "solution that fits" to reinforce control.

Risks to watch:

- Some product explanations can become dense when many benefit claims appear in one sentence.
- Claims like funding speed, offer limits, rates, and eligibility should stay qualified and footnoted.

## HEI Page Observations

Representative language:

- "Flexible, 30-year term"
- "No monthly payments"
- "No income requirements"
- "Keep the home you love"
- "Get freedom to breathe"
- "Freedom to dream"
- "Point's HEI is a partnership between you and Point."
- "You control when you exit the partnership during the 30-year term."
- "If your home's value goes up, Point shares in the gain. If the value falls, your buy back costs may be smaller."

Voice takeaways:

- HEI copy is strongest when it uses the partnership frame and explains the tradeoff clearly.
- The page pairs emotional benefits with practical use cases: debt, business, expenses, renovation, medical bills, education.
- The "freedom to breathe" and "freedom to dream" structure gives the product a human reason to exist.
- Cost examples are important to preserve trust because HEI is less familiar than traditional lending.

Risks to watch:

- HEI should not be simplified into "cash with no payments" without explaining the future buyback.
- "No monthly payments" needs context: repayment still happens when the homeowner exits, sells, refinances, or buys back the investment.

## HELOC Page Observations

Representative language:

- "HELOCs have more requirements than our flagship product, the Home Equity Investment (HEI), but they can be a great choice for well-qualified homeowners."
- "Get up to $750k with a HELOC"
- "Get cash in as little as 5 days"
- "Fast and easy online application"
- "Premium phone support"
- "Both the HELOC and the HEI offer cash from your home equity, a 30-year term with no prepayment penalty, and an easy online application, but there are some key differences."
- "You are not charged on your entire line of credit, only the funds you actually take out."

Voice takeaways:

- HELOC copy should be more direct and product-comparison oriented because the audience is likely comparing familiar credit products.
- The page appropriately states that HELOCs have stricter requirements.
- Strong language clarifies how a line of credit works and how payments are calculated.

Risks to watch:

- Keep availability current by state.
- Keep credit score requirements accurate.
- Avoid implying that every homeowner can access HELOC speed, amounts, or rates.

## Trust Signals

Across pages, Point leans on:

- Trustpilot rating and review volume.
- Customer quotes about clear communication, transparency, support, speed, and feeling informed.
- Press mentions about category innovation.
- Online process, dashboard visibility, and guided support.

Use trust signals to support claims, not replace product explanation.

## Brand Voice Summary

Point should sound like a knowledgeable home equity guide:

- It understands the homeowner's financial pressure.
- It explains unfamiliar products without talking down.
- It makes tradeoffs visible.
- It gives the homeowner a next step with low pressure.
- It treats home equity as something earned, valuable, and personal.
